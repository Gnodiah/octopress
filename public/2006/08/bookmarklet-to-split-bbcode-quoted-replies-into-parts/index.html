Bookmarklet to split BBCode quoted replies into parts

Written August 6, 2006 at 00:26 CEST. Tagged Bookmarklets, Firefox, JavaScript.

Every once in a while, I want to reply to a forum post part by part. For the common BBCode forums, doing this manually is a tedious matter of repeatedly typing and/or copy-and-pasting [QUOTE] and [/QUOTE] tags. An obvious candidate for automation.

I figured the best vehicle for this piece of magic would be a bookmarklet.

I can initiate a quoted reply, put “-snip-” or “-s-” wherever I want a word in, and then run the bookmarklet. It will insert the appropriate start and end tags, including any attributes to the start tag (e.g. [QUOTE="Squid"] or [QUOTE from="Squid"]), newlines to make room for my reply (after first removing superfluous whitespace around the snip point), and then put the caret in the first such slot.
